Beef Stew

Description

Embrace the heartwarming essence of homemade beef stew, a cherished comfort dish that combines tender stewing beef with an array of vegetables and rich flavors. Begin by coating succulent beef cubes with a delicate layer of all-purpose flour, a preparation that contributes to the stew's sumptuous consistency. In a generously sized pot, transform the floured beef into a golden symphony, each cube meticulously browned to enhance depth of flavor. Set aside the seared beef and usher in the next layer of aromatic delight with sautéed onions and softened carrots, each component revealing its unique character. Elevate the ensemble with minced garlic, a mere hint that blossoms into a fragrant revelation. Reunite the beef with the pot, and introduce cubed potatoes to complete the medley. A harmonious blend of beef broth, tomato paste, and Worcestershire sauce cascades, embracing the ingredients in a tantalizing embrace. Dried thyme and bay leaves join the performance, imparting an herbal symphony that evokes memories of cozy kitchens. As the stew simmers, the flavors meld and evolve, yielding a tender landscape of beef and vegetables that beckons with every spoonful. Optional frozen peas make a late appearance, their vibrant hue and tender nature preserving their essence in the final masterpiece. The stew's culmination, marked by fork-tender beef and a harmonious marriage of textures, awaits your indulgence. A sprinkle of chopped parsley crowns each serving, infusing the dish with a burst of freshness. Accompany this culinary triumph with crusty bread or a bed of rice, and savor the comfort and nourishment that only homemade beef stew can provide.

Ingredients

  • Stewing beef (cubed)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Olive oil
  • Onion (chopped)
  • Carrots (sliced)
  • Potatoes (cubed)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Beef broth
  • Tomato paste
  • Worcestershire sauce
  • Thyme (dried or fresh)
  • Bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper
  • Frozen peas (optional)
  • Chopped parsley (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Coat cubed stewing beef in all-purpose flour to thicken the stew. Shake off excess flour.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Brown floured beef cubes on all sides. Browned beef should be set aside.
  3. Sauté chopped onion and sliced carrots in the same pot until onion is translucent and carrots soften.
  4. Add minced garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Return browned beef cubes to the pot, and add cubed potatoes.
  6. Pour in beef broth, tomato paste, and Worcestershire sauce. Stir to combine.
  7. Introduce dried or fresh thyme and bay leaves. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours until beef is tender and flavors meld.
  9. Add frozen peas in the last 10 minutes of cooking, if desired.
  10. Remove bay leaves, taste, and adjust seasoning.
  11. Ladle stew into bowls and garnish with chopped parsley.
  12. Serve with crusty bread or over rice for a warming meal.
